## Strategy 1: Home Office Deduction 🏠

**Status:** ⚠️ READY TO IMPLEMENT
**Annual saving:** ~£2,295 (corp tax) + reduces personal draw needed

### Calculation

| Item | Monthly | Business % | Claimable £/mo | Annual |
|------|---------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Rent | £2,650 | 33% | £875 | £10,494 |
| Council tax | £150 | 33% | £50 | £594 |
| Utilities | £250 | 33% | £83 | £990 |
| **Total** | | | **£1,007** | **£12,078** |

**Basis:** 2-bed flat, 3 usable rooms (kitchen, bedroom, office). Office used exclusively for business ~16hrs/day, 7 days/week.

**Method:** Room proportion (1/3 = 33%). HMRC accepts this when room is used exclusively for business.

**⚠️ Capital gains risk:** If a room is used "exclusively" for business, that portion of the property may be subject to CGT on sale. In practice:
- Jamie rents (doesn't own), so CGT is NOT an issue currently
- If/when buying a house, revisit this — may want to claim "mainly" not "exclusively" to preserve full PPR relief

**Garden as business space:** Jamie uses garden for client workouts. This could increase the claim BUT needs careful documentation:
- Log client sessions held in garden (dates, times, client names)
- Take photos of setup
- Consider it as additional business-use space — increases the % claim
- Conservative approach: don't increase % but claim garden-specific costs (equipment, maintenance) as business expenses
- Aggressive approach: count garden as a 4th "room" → 2/4 = 50% claim
- **Recommendation:** Discuss with accountant. Document everything regardless.

**Status:** 🔴 NEEDS LEGAL REVIEW — COMPLEX

**The opportunity:** Issue shares to Saida → she receives dividends → uses her own:
- Personal allowance: £12,570
- Dividend allowance: £1,000
- Basic rate band: up to £50,270

**Potential saving:** If Saida has no other income, she could receive ~£13,570 tax-free (salary equivalent + dividend allowance). Plus dividends up to £50,270 at just 8.75%.

**⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING — Settlements Legislation:**
- HMRC can challenge dividend splitting with unmarried partners under ITTOIA 2005 s624-628
- The "Arctic Systems" case (Jones v Garnett) created an exemption for married couples — but NOT unmarried partners
- If Saida has no genuine role in the business, HMRC may treat this as a "settlement" and tax Jamie on the dividends instead
- **Mitigation:** Saida needs a genuine role (admin, social media, client management, bookkeeping — anything real)
- This needs a proper conversation with the accountant

## Strategy 6: Luxury Gym as Business Expense 🏋️

**Status:** ⚠️ POSSIBLE BUT NEEDS CAREFUL FRAMING

**The argument:** Jamie chooses a luxury gym specifically because potential coaching clients frequent it. It's a networking/business development venue, not personal fitness.

**HMRC position:** Gym membership is generally a personal expense. BUT:
- If the gym is genuinely used for client meetings/networking → marketing expense
- If clients are coached there → business premises cost
- Key: the PRIMARY purpose must be business, not personal benefit
- **Dual purpose rule:** If something has both personal and business benefit, HMRC says it's NOT deductible (unlike US rules)

**Mitigation:**
- Keep a log of business activities at the gym (client meetings, networking contacts)
- If >50% of gym visits involve client/business activity, stronger claim
- Consider: claim only the difference between cheap gym (personal) and luxury gym (business premium)
- Better: if training clients at the gym, it's a business premises cost — much cleaner

**Recommendation:** Discuss with accountant. If training clients there, strong case. If just networking, weaker case.
