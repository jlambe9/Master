## 3. Personalisation Levels by Sequence Step

### Intro (Day 1) — Hyper-personalised

- **AI gen:** ~5s | **Your review:** 30–60s | **Edit rate:** 30–40% | **Template:** No
- Unique to this person. AI researches their profile, recent posts, company, role changes.

**Hooks:** Recent post/article, role change or promotion, company news/funding, mutual connection, specific pain point from bio.

**Bad:** "Hi [Name], I help founders with mental performance. Would love to connect and share some insights!"

**Good:** "Hi [Name] — saw your post about the 3am debugging sessions after your Series A close. That 'wired but exhausted' pattern is something I work on specifically with ADHD founders. Curious — have you noticed it getting worse since the raise?"

**Why it works:** References specific content, names the exact pattern, asks a question that validates their experience. They feel seen, not sold to.

### FU1 (Day 3) — Hyper-personalised

- **AI gen:** ~5s | **Your review:** 20–40s | **Edit rate:** 20–30% | **Template:** No
- Different angle from intro. AI picks a second hook. Must not repeat the intro's angle.

**Hooks:** Different post/content than intro, industry-specific challenge, team size/scaling pain, shared interest, value-add resource.

**Bad:** "Hey [Name], just following up on my last message. Would love to chat about how I can help with your mental performance."

**Good:** "Btw [Name] — came across something you might find useful. We ran data on 40 ADHD founders and the #1 predictor of burnout wasn't workload, it was sleep architecture collapse in months 2–4 post-raise. Happy to share the brief if you're curious."

**Why it works:** New value (data/research), specific to their situation (post-raise), gives before asking. The intro asked a question; this offers something.

### FU2 (Day 7) — Semi-templated

- **AI gen:** ~2s | **Your review:** 10–15s (spot check) | **Edit rate:** 10–15% | **Template:** Yes
- Template structure with 1–2 AI-filled variables. Value-add message.

**Hooks:** Case study result, relevant resource/tool, social proof from similar persona.

**Bad:** "Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up again. We've helped lots of founders improve their focus. Let me know if you'd like to chat."

**Good:** "Quick one [Name] — just finished a 6-week run with a [similar role] at a [similar stage] company. They went from 4hrs of deep work per week to 14 by week 5. Wrote up the protocol if you want a look."

**Why it works:** Template structure (just finished... went from X to Y... wrote up...) but variables are AI-filled from profile data. Feels personal, costs 2s to generate.

### FU3 (Day 11) — Templated

- **AI gen:** ~1s | **Your review:** 0 (batch send) | **Edit rate:** 0% | **Template:** Yes
- Variable CTA. Different ask — not harder, different.

**Hooks:** Different CTA format, scarcity/timing, direct question.

**Bad:** "Hey [Name], just bumping this to the top of your inbox. Let me know if you're interested."

**Good:** "Honest question [Name] — is cognitive performance something you're actively working on, or more of a 'someday' thing? Either way is fine, just helps me know whether to keep sharing stuff or leave you in peace."

**Why it works:** Permission-based, zero pressure, forces a binary response. Even "someday" is useful data. Even "leave me alone" is a reply.

### Breakup (Day 14) — Templated

- **AI gen:** ~1s | **Your review:** 0 (batch send) | **Edit rate:** 0% | **Template:** Yes
- Permission-based close. Recovers 3–5% of dead threads.

**Hooks:** Permission to close, open door for future.

**Bad:** "Last chance [Name]! Don't miss out on this opportunity to transform your cognitive performance!"

**Good:** "No worries if the timing's off [Name] — I'll stop filling your inbox. If cognitive performance becomes a priority down the line, I'm easy to find. All the best with [specific thing from their profile]."

**Why it works:** Graceful exit. References something specific (shows you're human). People reply to breakups because the pressure is gone.

## 4. Factors Affecting Each Funnel Component

### Accept Rate (CR → Accept: 30–70%)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Headline / Title | ★★★★★ | First thing they see. Job title + value prop > just title |
| Profile Photo | ★★★★☆ | Professional, face visible, high-trust. No logos, no sunglasses |
| Mutual Connections | ★★★★☆ | Social proof at a glance. 10+ mutuals = 2× accept rate |
| Banner Image | ★★★☆☆ | Reinforces positioning. Authority signal if done well |
| CR Note vs Blank | ★★☆☆☆ | Debated — blank often outperforms bad copy. Good personalised note wins |
| ICP Targeting | ★★★★★ | Wrong audience = low accept regardless of everything else |
| SSI Score | ★★★☆☆ | 70+ opens algorithmic reach and higher CR limits |
| Day of Week | ★★☆☆☆ | Tues–Thurs outperforms weekends on accept rate |

### Intro Reply Rate (Accept → First Reply: 10–14%)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Personalisation Depth | ★★★★★ | Generic vs hyper-personalised = 2–3× difference |
| Speed to Lead | ★★★★★ | Within 1hr of accept vs 24hr+ → reply rate drops ~50% |
| Message Structure | ★★★★☆ | Question-led vs pitch-led. Open loops > closed statements |
| Offer Clarity | ★★★★☆ | "What's in it for me" within 2 sentences or lost |
| Profile Depth (when they click) | ★★★☆☆ | Articles, posts, About section validate or kill credibility |
| Message Length | ★★★☆☆ | 2–4 sentences optimal. Over 150 words = unread |

### FU Sequence Reply Rate (Remaining → Reply: 12–20% cumulative)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Personalised FU1 vs Templated | ★★★★★ | 2× reply rate difference. Worth the effort |
| Passive Touches Between FUs | ★★★★☆ | Profile views + post likes warm before next message |
| Angle Variation | ★★★★☆ | Same ask repeated = ignored. New value angle each FU |
| Timing / Cadence | ★★★☆☆ | Too fast = spam, too slow = forgotten. 2–4 day gaps optimal |
| Your Content Activity | ★★★☆☆ | If they see your posts in feed between FUs = massive warm effect |
| Breakup Message | ★★☆☆☆ | Permission-based close. Recovers 3–5% of dead sequences |

### Conversation → Call Booked (Reply → Calendar: 20–30%)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Offer Specificity | ★★★★★ | "15-min cognitive audit" > "let's chat". Concrete = booked |
| Booking Friction | ★★★★☆ | Calendly link vs back-and-forth = 2× booking rate |
| Social Proof in Convo | ★★★★☆ | Case studies / numbers dropped naturally in chat |
| Website / Landing Page | ★★★☆☆ | They will check before committing. Bad site = no book |
| Transition Timing | ★★★☆☆ | Ask too early = pushy. Too late = momentum lost. 2–3 exchanges sweet spot |

### Call Showed (Booked → Showed: 70–80%)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation Sequence | ★★★★★ | Reminder 24hr + 1hr before. SMS > email for show rate |
| Perceived Value | ★★★★☆ | "Free cognitive assessment" > "discovery call" |
| Ease of Access | ★★★☆☆ | Zoom link in calendar invite, no login walls, no friction |
| Time Gap | ★★★☆☆ | Book within 48hrs. >5 days out = no-show rate doubles |

### Call → Closed (Showed → Closed: 20–30%)

| Factor | Impact | Note |
|---|---|---|
| ICP Fit (set upstream) | ★★★★★ | Wrong person = no close regardless of call quality |
| Call Structure / Framework | ★★★★★ | Diagnosis → prescription → offer. Not pitch → objection handle |
| Objection Handling | ★★★★☆ | Pre-empt top 3 objections before they arise |
| Offer-Market Fit | ★★★★☆ | Does your offer solve a problem they actually feel? |
| Price Anchoring | ★★★☆☆ | Frame against cost of inaction, not competitor pricing |

## 5. Diagnostic Cheat Sheet

### Low Accept Rate (<35%)

**Symptoms:** High CR volume but few new connections · Pending requests piling up · LinkedIn warnings about connection limits

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| Headline reads like a job title, not a value prop | Rewrite: "[Outcome] for [audience]" format |
| Targeting too broad / wrong ICP | Tighten filters: industry + seniority + company size |
| Low mutual connections with target audience | Connect with adjacent people first to build social proof |
| Profile photo looks amateur or corporate stock | Professional headshot, natural background, approachable expression |
| Sending CRs on weekends | Shift volume to Tues–Thurs. Queue weekends for passive touches |
| High "I don't know this person" reports | Add personalised CR notes OR reduce volume to better-targeted ICPs |

### Low Intro Reply Rate (<8%)

**Symptoms:** Connections accept but conversations don't start · High "seen" rate but no replies · Replies are polite shutdowns

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| Sending pitch on intro instead of starting conversation | Lead with observation/question, not offer |
| Messaging 24hr+ after accept | Automate or batch intros within 1hr of accept |
| Generic "great to connect" openers | Reference their content, role change, mutual connection, or recent post |
| Message too long (>150 words) | Cut to 2–4 sentences. One question. One reason to reply |
| About section / profile doesn't back up the intro | Audit what they see when they click your name. Align messaging |

### Low FU Reply Rate (<10% cumulative)

**Symptoms:** First message gets some traction but sequence dies · FU2+ getting zero replies · People unconnecting or marking as spam

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| All FUs repeat the same ask | Different angle each time: value, social proof, scarcity, question |
| FU1 is templated / not personalised | Hyper-personalise FU1 — it's your highest-leverage FU |
| No passive touches between messages | Add profile view (Day 2) + post like (Day 5) between FUs |
| Cadence too aggressive (<24hr gaps) | Space FUs 2–4 days apart minimum |
| No content activity on your profile | Post 2–3×/week. Feed presence warms between FUs passively |

### Low Call Booking Rate (<15%)

**Symptoms:** Good conversations that go nowhere · People say "sounds interesting" then ghost · Scheduling back-and-forth that fizzles

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| Vague CTA: "let's chat sometime" | Specific: "15-min [specific audit/assessment] — here's my calendar" |
| No Calendly / booking link | Eliminate friction. One click to book |
| Asking too early (first reply) | Build 2–3 exchanges of rapport/value before the ask |
| No social proof in conversation | Drop a case study or result naturally: "worked with someone similar who..." |
| Website/landing page undermines credibility | Audit your site. If it's weak, use a Calendly page with testimonials instead |

### Low Show Rate (<65%)

**Symptoms:** Calendar full of bookings, half empty on the day · Last-minute cancellations · No-shows with no explanation

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| No confirmation/reminder sequence | 24hr + 1hr reminders. SMS + email. Include value prop reminder |
| Booking too far out (>5 days) | Offer slots within 48hrs. Urgency = attendance |
| Low perceived value of the call | Reframe: "assessment" / "audit" with deliverable, not "discovery call" |
| Friction to join (complex login, app download) | Simple Zoom link, no passwords, in calendar invite |

### Low Close Rate (<15%)

**Symptoms:** Good calls that end with "let me think about it" · Price objections dominating · Prospects excited but don't convert

| Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|
| Pitching before diagnosing | Structure: 60% diagnosis, 20% prescription, 20% offer |
| ICP misfit — right conversation, wrong person | Tighten upstream targeting. Qualify harder in conversation stage |
| No urgency / cost of inaction | Quantify what staying the same costs them. Make status quo painful |
| Price presented without anchoring | Anchor against cost of problem, not competitor pricing |
| No clear next step on the call | End with specific action + timeline, not "I'll send info" |

## 7. Global Levers (Ranked by Compound Impact)

### 🔴 HIGHEST Impact

**ICP Targeting Precision**
Affects: Accept Rate, Intro Reply, Close Rate
Every upstream improvement compounds downstream. Moving from "founders" to "ADHD founders in tech, 50–200 employees, raised Series A+" changes every number.
Effort: 2–4 hrs research + filter setup

**Speed to Lead (Automation)**
Affects: Intro Reply Rate, Conversation Rate
Messaging within 1hr vs 24hr is the single biggest operational lever. Requires automation or rigid daily batching habit.
Effort: Tool setup or daily 30min block

### 🟡 HIGH Impact

**Personalisation System**
Affects: Intro Reply, FU1 Reply, Conversation Quality
Build a repeatable system: 3 personalisation hooks per prospect (recent post, role change, company news). Template the structure, personalise the variables.
Effort: Design once, 2min/prospect ongoing

**Content Engine (3×/week posting)**
Affects: Accept Rate, FU Reply Rate, Call Booking, Brand Equity
Consistent posting warms prospects passively. They see you in feed before and between messages. Compounds over 60–90 days.
Effort: 3–4 hrs/week

**Offer Crystallisation**
Affects: Intro Reply, Call Booking, Close Rate
"6-week Brain Battery for ADHD founders — 83% report improved focus by week 3" > "coaching".
Effort: Half-day workshop with yourself

### 🔵 MEDIUM Impact

**Confirmation / Reminder Sequence**
Affects: Show Rate only (but each saved no-show = real revenue)
Automated SMS + email at 24hr + 1hr. Restate value prop. Include Zoom link.
Effort: 1hr automation setup

**Call Framework / Script**
Affects: Close Rate
Diagnosis → prescription → offer. Record calls, review weekly.
Effort: Half-day to build, ongoing refinement

**Website / Landing Page**
Affects: Call Booking, Close Rate (validation step)
Prospects check your site before booking. Focused landing page with testimonials + clear offer > a full site.
Effort: 1–2 days

**A/B Testing Intros (3 variants)**
Affects: Intro Reply Rate (then cascades)
Run 3 intro variants, 50 sends each. Pick winner. Repeat monthly. 10% → 14% intro reply = 40% more conversations.
Effort: 1 week per cycle

### 🟣 LOW-MEDIUM Impact

**LinkedIn SSI Optimisation**
Affects: CR Limits, Algorithmic Reach, Accept Rate
SSI 70+ unlocks higher CR limits and better feed visibility. Driven by profile completeness, posting, engagement, network growth.
Effort: Byproduct of doing other things well
